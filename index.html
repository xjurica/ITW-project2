PACIFIC CREST TRAIL

About PCT

Journey

The Pacific Crest Trail (PCT) is one of the most iconic long-distance hiking trails in the world, stretching approximately 2,650 miles (4,265 kilometers) from the border of Mexico to the border of Canada. It passes through three states: California, Oregon, and Washington.

Diversity

The trail winds its way through a stunning variety of landscapes, including the arid deserts of Southern California, the majestic peaks of the Sierra Nevada and Cascade ranges, lush forests, and scenic alpine meadows.

Hikers

Thousands of hikers from around the world are drawn to the challenge and adventure of the PCT each year. Some attempt to hike the entire trail in one continuous journey, known as thru-hiking, which typically takes five to six months to complete. Others opt for section hiking, tackling smaller segments of the trail.

Along the way, hikers encounter a supportive community of fellow outdoor enthusiasts, known as the "PCT family", who share stories, advice, and camaraderie. Trail towns located near the PCT provide resupply points, lodging, and other amenities, making it possible for hikers to rest and replenish their supplies during their journey.

Exploring PCT Terrain

Embark on a journey through the diverse landscapes of the Pacific Crest Trail (PCT). In this section, we'll guide you through the trail's stages, each offering its own unique natural wonders and challenges. From the sun-baked deserts of Southern California to the towering peaks of the Sierra Nevada and the lush forests of Washington, the PCT promises an unforgettable adventure for all who tread its path.

Southern California Desert

Stage 1: Southern California Desert

Starting Point: Southern Terminus

The journey begins at the southern terminus of the PCT, located near the border of Mexico. Hikers are greeted by the stark beauty of the desert landscape, characterized by rolling hills, scrubby vegetation, and expansive vistas. The desert section of the trail is known for its extreme temperatures, with scorching heat during the day and chilly nights. Hikers must carry ample water and supplies to navigate this challenging terrain.

Natural Beauty of the Desert

Despite the harsh conditions, the desert offers its own unique beauty, with colorful sunsets, blooming wildflowers, and the chance to spot wildlife such as lizards, snakes, and birds. Hikers can experience the solitude and tranquility of the desert, with its vast open spaces and starry night skies.

Water Availability

In addition to the challenges posed by the desert terrain, water availability is a critical concern for hikers on the Pacific Crest Trail.

The Pacific Crest Trail Association (PCTA) publishes a Water Report that provides up-to-date information on water sources along the trail. This invaluable resource is maintained by volunteers and hikers who report on the status of springs, streams, and other water sources, including their flow rate and reliability.

Hikers rely on the Water Report to make informed decisions about where to find water and how much to carry between water sources. It's essential to stay hydrated, so hikers carefully plan their daily mileage and water stops based on the information provided in the Water Report.

Sierra Nevada

Discover the Majesty of the Sierra Nevada

The Sierra Nevada section of the Pacific Crest Trail offers hikers an unforgettable journey through some of the most stunning mountain landscapes in the United States. Stretching hundreds of miles, this portion of the trail traverses rugged peaks, pristine alpine lakes, and lush forests.

Key highlights of the Sierra Nevada include:

  • Majestic Peaks: Explore iconic peaks such as Mount Whitney, the highest point in the contiguous United States, and the jagged spires of the Minarets.
  • Alpine Lakes: Discover crystal-clear alpine lakes nestled amidst granite cliffs, perfect for a refreshing dip or scenic picnic.
  • Sequoia Groves: Wander through ancient groves of giant sequoias, some of the largest and oldest trees on Earth.
  • Wildlife Encounters: Keep an eye out for wildlife such as black bears, deer, and marmots as you traverse the Sierra Nevada's diverse ecosystems.

Whether you're seeking breathtaking vistas, challenging terrain, or simply a deep connection with nature, the Sierra Nevada section of the PCT has something to offer every hiker.

Cascade Mountains

Exploring the Cascade Mountains

The Cascade Mountains, stretching from British Columbia through Washington, Oregon, and into Northern California, form a breathtaking natural barrier and offer a diverse range of experiences for hikers on the Pacific Crest Trail.

Key highlights of the Cascade Mountains section include:

Volcanic Peaks

Explore iconic volcanic peaks such as Mount Rainier, Mount Hood, and Mount Shasta, each offering unique challenges and stunning views.

Evergreen Forests

Traverse dense forests of Douglas fir, western red cedar, and hemlock, home to a variety of wildlife including elk, black bears, and mountain goats.

Glacial Lakes

Discover crystal-clear glacial lakes nestled among the rugged peaks, offering opportunities for swimming, fishing, and camping in pristine wilderness settings.

Waterfalls

Marvel at spectacular waterfalls such as Snoqualmie Falls, Proxy Falls, and Burney Falls, cascading from towering cliffs and adding to the region's scenic beauty.

Whether you're drawn to the challenge of summiting volcanic peaks, exploring ancient forests, or simply immersing yourself in the beauty of cascading waterfalls, the Cascade Mountains section of the PCT offers an unforgettable adventure.

Washington

Exploring the Natural Wonders of Washington

Washington State, nestled in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States, offers a diverse array of natural landscapes and outdoor adventures along the Pacific Crest Trail.

Key highlights of the Washington section include:

Olympic National Park

Explore the stunning Olympic National Park, home to lush rainforests, rugged coastline, and towering peaks. Witness the diversity of ecosystems, from temperate rainforests to alpine meadows.

Mount Rainier

Marvel at the iconic Mount Rainier, an active stratovolcano and the highest peak in Washington. Experience breathtaking views, alpine meadows, and pristine wilderness surrounding the majestic mountain.

Columbia River Gorge

Discover the scenic beauty of the Columbia River Gorge, where the mighty Columbia River cuts through the Cascade Mountains. Hike among waterfalls, towering cliffs, and lush forests in this dramatic landscape.

North Cascades National Park

Immerse yourself in the rugged wilderness of North Cascades National Park, known as the "American Alps." Explore jagged peaks, glacier-carved valleys, and pristine alpine lakes in this remote landscape.

Whether you're captivated by the towering peaks, enchanted by the lush forests, or mesmerized by the cascading waterfalls, the Washington section of the PCT promises an unforgettable journey through some of the Pacific Northwest's most spectacular landscapes.
